Assessment Operating Model

Evidence-linked

Each criterion result includes a page reference, evidence depth statement, and reasoning that can be reviewed by the assessor.

Level-aware judgement

The prompts now distinguish the expected quality of explanation, analysis, and evaluation across Levels 3, 4 and 5.

Review-first safety

Thin, ambiguous, or authenticity-risk evidence is marked for review instead of forcing a confident grade claim.

Evidence-Based Assessment Principles

Judge what is present

Feedback should be based on demonstrated evidence in the submitted work, not what the learner may have intended to show.

Match the command verb

Describe, explain, analyse and evaluate are not interchangeable. Achievement depends on the actual demand of the criterion.

Protect threshold accuracy

Pass must be secure before Merit is considered, and Merit must be secure before Distinction is claimed.

Support useful feedback

Actions should tell the learner what kind of missing evidence, depth or precision is needed next.
